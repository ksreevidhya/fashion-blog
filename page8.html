Consumer Segments: Understanding the Market Through His Work

Studying his audience helped me understand real consumer psychology:

  • Brides seeking heirloom couture
  • High-net-worth collectors who value textiles
  • NRIs wanting authentic cultural luxury
  • Gen Z inspired by his digital storytelling
  • Men choosing khadi bandhgalas and brocade sherwanis

It showed me that craft adapts beautifully across income levels, aspirations, and lifestyle segments.

⸻

Conclusion: My Personal Takeaway

This research changed the way I understand fashion. Sabyasachi’s work taught me that design isn’t only about trends—it’s about respect for craft, patience in process, and the emotions woven into handmade textiles.

Through this study, I learned to see the beauty in handloom irregularities, the discipline in zardozi precision, the stories inside block prints, and the humanity behind every artisan’s touch.

Most importantly, I realised that Indian luxury is not about extravagance—it is about heritage, time, and craftsmanship. And as a fashion student, that is a lesson I will carry into everything I create.
